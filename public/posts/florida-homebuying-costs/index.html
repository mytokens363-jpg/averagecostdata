Florida Homebuying Costs: Taxes, Insurance & Market Realities

A breakdown of Florida’s true cost of homeownership—property taxes, insurance, regional price disparities, and key financial considerations for buyers.
April 11, 2026 · 2 min · Average Cost Data

Florida remains one of the most sought-after destinations for homebuyers in the United States, driven by a booming economy, no state income tax, and a lifestyle that appeals to retirees and young professionals alike. However, the Sunshine State presents a unique financial landscape that requires careful navigation. While the absence of state income tax is a significant draw, the cost of housing and associated living expenses can vary dramatically depending on your chosen location. Understanding the true cost of ownership involves more than just securing a mortgage; it requires a deep dive into insurance markets, property tax structures, and daily operational costs. Florida property tax exemptions For anyone considering a move to the peninsula, a comprehensive breakdown of these financial realities is essential for long-term stability.

The Current Florida Real Estate Landscape#

The Florida housing market has experienced significant volatility over the past few years. Following a surge in demand during the pandemic, the market has adjusted to rising interest rates, yet prices in many coastal regions remain stubbornly high. As of early 2024, the median home price across the state hovers around $430,000. This figure, however, masks the extreme disparities between metropolitan hubs and rural inland communities. Buyers need to be prepared for competitive bidding in desirable areas, even as inventory levels slowly recover from historic lows.

Median Home Prices by Metro Area#

Location plays the most critical role in determining your initial capital outlay. In South Florida, particularly Miami-Dade and Broward counties, the median price often exceeds $600,000 for a single-family home. This premium is driven by high demand from international buyers and limited land availability. Conversely, Central Florida offers a more moderate entry point. In the Orlando metropolitan area, the median home price sits closer to $420,000, while Tampa Bay averages around $410,000
