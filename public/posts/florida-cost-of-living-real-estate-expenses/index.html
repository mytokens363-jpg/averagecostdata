Cost of Living in Florida: Real Estate & Hidden Expenses

Explore Florida housing costs, regional price trends, and hidden homeownership expenses like property taxes, insurance, and utilities.
April 11, 2026 · 4 min · Average Cost Data

Florida remains one of the most attractive destinations for retirees, remote workers, and families seeking a tax-friendly environment. However, the Sunshine State’s real estate landscape is complex, with costs fluctuating significantly depending on geography and lifestyle choices. Understanding the true cost of living in Florida requires looking beyond the sticker price of a home. It involves analyzing property taxes, insurance premiums, utility costs, and regional market trends.

Whether you are eyeing a condo in Miami or a single-family home in Jacksonville, financial preparedness is key. This guide breaks down the current housing market data to help you budget accurately for your move to Florida.

Florida Real Estate Market Overview#

The Florida housing market has experienced volatile shifts over the last few years. Interest rate changes and inventory shortages have influenced pricing, though the state continues to see robust demand due to population migration. As of early 2024, the median home price across the state hovers around $410,000. This figure represents a moderate increase from previous years, stabilizing after the pandemic-era surge.

Median Home Prices by Region#

Prices vary drastically between coastal hotspots and inland areas. In the Miami-Fort Lauderdale-West Palm Beach metropolitan area, the median listing price often exceeds $575,000. This premium is driven by high demand for waterfront properties and urban luxury living. Conversely, inland regions offer more affordability. For example, the median home price in the Jacksonville area sits closer to $340,000, making it a popular choice for first-time buyers.

Central Florida presents a middle ground. Orlando and Tampa Bay areas typically see median prices ranging from $380,000 to $420,000. These markets benefit from strong job growth in tourism and healthcare sectors, keeping demand steady. Florida housing market trends

Market Trends and Inventory#

Inventory levels remain a critical factor for buyers. Historically, Florida has struggled with low housing stock, which keeps prices elevated. In 2023, months of supply hovered around 2.5 months, indicating a seller’s market. A balanced market typically sits at 6 months of supply. Consequently, homes in desirable school districts or beachfront locations often receive multiple offers within days of listing.

Hidden Costs of Homeownership#

Many new residents focus solely on the mortgage payment, overlooking the substantial recurring costs associated with owning property in Florida. These additional expenses can increase your monthly housing budget by 30% to 50% compared to the national average.

Property Taxes and Insurance#

Florida does not levy a state income tax, but property taxes and insurance costs can offset that benefit. The average effective property tax rate in Florida is approximately 0.98%. For a $400,000 home, this translates to roughly $3,920 annually or $327 monthly. Homestead exemptions can reduce this burden for primary residents, capping the assessed value increase at 3% per year.

Insurance is the most volatile cost. Due to hurricane risks and litigation issues, homeowners insurance rates are among the highest in the nation. The average annual premium in Florida is approximately $4,500, compared to the national average of $1,700. In high-risk coastal counties, premiums can exceed $6,000 to $8,000 per year without a flood policy. Florida homeowners insurance rates

Maintenance and Utilities#

Maintenance costs in Florida are elevated due to the humid subtropical climate. Air conditioning systems run frequently, leading to higher utility bills. The average monthly electricity bill for a 2,000-square-foot home ranges from $200 to $350 during summer months. Additionally, pest control and pool maintenance (common for single-family homes) add $100 to $200 monthly.

Plumbing and structural maintenance require vigilance due to soil composition and humidity. Budgeting 1% of the home’s value annually for repairs is a standard industry recommendation. For a median-priced home, this equals $4,100 per year.

Rental Market Dynamics#

For those not ready to buy, the rental market offers flexibility but comes with its own price pressures. The median rent for a two-bedroom apartment in Florida is approximately $2,100 per month.

  • Miami: Median 2-bed rent is $2,850.
  • Orlando: Median 2-bed rent is $2,000.
  • Tampa: Median 2-bed rent is $2,150.
  • Jacksonville: Median 2-bed rent is $1,750.

Rental demand remains high, particularly in urban centers where job opportunities are concentrated. Many landlords now require credit scores above 680 and income verification at 3x the monthly rent. Florida rental market analysis
